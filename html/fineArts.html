Behind the Doors

Through this piece I wanted to embody the feelings that I went through, during an unpleasant incident that took place when I was in primary school. The closed windows mimic the ones present in my high school while the young girl in the keyhole is symbolic of my feelings of shame and disbelief after I realized what had happened.

Collage

The collage is based on a photograph I took of a chaotic scene in Byculla, one of the busiest streets of my hometown. I chose to collage this photograph as it further emphasizes the confusion and chaos in the scene and the daily lives of the residents of my hometown.

Preserving Culture

This painting depicts the importance of preserving Indiaʼs rich heritage and culture in a city that is otherwise accustomed to both westernization and modernization.



Inauguration

Inauguration is a painting of an x-ray of my motherʼs hip girdle and of a fetus. In India the practice of female infanticide is still rampant in remote areas. This so called “tradition” reinforces age old stereotypes, that the female child is a financial burden on the family. Through this painting I have drawn a ribbon symbolic of an umbilical cord which is being cut to inaugurate and welcome every female child into this world. The background has important words from an article about this controversial issues that have been filled in with glue in order to give the painting a third dimension.
